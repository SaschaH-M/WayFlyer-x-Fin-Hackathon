How StockSense works

A transparent scoring formula over 24 months of real sales data — no machine learning, no black box. Every recommendation is reproducible and traces back to specific rows of data.
Not a trained model. StockSense isn’t a neural network and nothing is “trained.’” It’s a deterministic calculation that runs identically every time — you could rebuild it in a spreadsheet. That means it can’t hallucinate, and a buyer can audit exactly why any SKU was flagged. The Backtest tab validates the formula (it doesn’t train it) by hiding the future and checking the calls against what really happened.

Step 1 · Measure how fast each variant sells

  • Recent rate = units sold in the last 3 months ÷ 13 weeks
  • Long-run rate = units sold in the last 12 months ÷ 52 weeks
  • Blended velocity = recent × 0.6 + long-run × 0.4 — leans toward what’s selling now, but one hot month can’t dominate
  • If a variant had no 12-month sales, it falls back to the 3-month rate (or zero)

Step 2 · Work out how long stock will last

  • Months of cover = inventory ÷ weekly velocity ÷ 4.33 (4.33 weeks per month)
  • No velocity but stock on hand → treated as infinite cover (dead stock)
  • Negative inventory (oversold) → shown as Out of Stock

Step 3 · Score it 0–100 (three parts added together)

  • Stock urgency (0–50): 50 if empty, 0 if more than 12 months of cover, sliding linearly in between — 50 × (1 − cover/12)
  • Demand intensity (0–30): this variant’s percentile rank by 12-month units vs all 645 others, × 30. Top seller ≈ 30, median ≈ 15. Percentile (not raw units) so a few blockbusters don’t crush everything else.
  • Trend bonus (0–20): extra points when the recent rate beats the long-run rate — (recent − long-run) / long-run × 50, capped at 20. Catches accelerating demand early.

Step 4 · Sort into an action

  • Inventory ≤ 0 → Reorder
  • More than 12 months of cover & still selling → Mark Down
  • Less than 2 months of cover & still selling → Reorder
  • Score ≥ 50 (but not the above) → Watch
  • Stock on hand but zero velocity → Mark Down (dead stock)
  • Everything else → Healthy

Step 5 · Cost every recommendation

  • Reorder quantity = ~12 weeks of velocity (or 80% of last-3-month units if no velocity)
  • Reorder cost = quantity × landed cost per unit
  • Markdown value = current stock × landed cost (the cash currently trapped)

The Backtest — validation, not training

Same formula, but it’s only shown data up to 31 May 2025 (end of month 12). It issues Reorder / Mark Down / Hold calls, then the real months 13–24 are replayed against them: actual stockouts from the inventory ledger, actual leftover stock from real sell-through. This proves the calls were right without the formula ever seeing the future.

Key assumptions & caveats

  • “Today” in the dataset is 1 June 2026; the data spans 24 months (Jun 2024 – May 2026), all synthetic.
  • Landed cost per variant comes from its most recent purchase order; variants with no PO use the average landed cost as a fallback.
  • Cash-flow scenarios — Conservative: 20% off, 50% sell-through, restock top 50% of flagged SKUs. Moderate: 30% / 70% / 80%. Aggressive: 40% / 85% / 100%.
  • Freed cash = markdown stock at retail × (1 − discount) × sell-through. It is not the cost-basis trapped-cash figure.
  • Revenue multiplier (8.34×) = annual stockout revenue run-rate ÷ total reorder cost. It assumes restocked demand returns to its prior level.
  • Backtest revenue is discounted by an 80% capture rate — we don’t claim to recover 100% of lost sales.
  • Lead times (~60–90 days) mean recovery isn’t instant: the backtest models deliveries starting ~2 months after a reorder, then ramping.
  • Monthly timing in the backtest is shaped by real seasonal factors (markdowns front-loaded, recovery delayed) — the 12-month totals are exact; the month-by-month split is modelled.
  • Cannibalization & seasonality aren’t fully removed: some “lost” sales may have shifted to in-stock substitutes, so recovery figures are a target, not a guarantee.
